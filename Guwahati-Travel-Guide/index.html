About Guwahati

Guwahati is the one of largest city of North East. It is also one of the fastest developing city. Guwahati plays a vital role in all the major trades in north east, and hece is also termed as the Trade Center of North East.

Guwahati is bestowed with natural beauty primarily because of its' location. It lies between the banks of Brahmaputra and foothills of Shillong Plateau.

Guwahati is also considered as a holy pilgrimage owing to presence of many temples of historical importance. Few amongst these are Kamakhya temple, Vasistha Temple etc.

Guwahati is also considered as a major educational hub of Assam and entire North East. Guwahati witnesses presence of some of the most premier insititutes across India. Some of these are IIT Guwahati, Gauhati University. It also is neighbor to a city which is gifted with an IIM i.e. shillong.

Temples

The Kamakhya Temple

The Kamakhya Temple, a pilgrimage centre of Hindus is located at a distance of 7 km from Guwahati. It is considered as a temple of "Shakti" (hindi word for Power). There are images of Ganesha (Hindu god of Management), Chamundeshwari(Hindu Goddess who killed devil named Chamund), and many other ancient dancing figures of various gods and goddesses.

It is considered very holy to visit Kamakhya Temple during Durga Puja which is celebrated annually during Navaratri in the month of September-October. Due to very huge number of tourists visiting the temple at this time, it becomes really difficult to get Darshan of the goddess. Hence, Guwahati Travel Guide helps devotees to get Darshan of the goddess so that all their wishes may come true.

Temples of Hajo

Devotees from different religions (Hindus, Buddhists, and Muslims) visit this ancient pilgrimage centre. It is also situated at the banks of mighty river Brahmaputra, and is 24 km. away from guwahati city. There are many temples nearby Monikut Hill (where all of these are situated) among which a Temple of Lord Shiva (Lord of Destruction (Vinashak)) known as Kedareswara Temple is most famous. Some other temples which also hold significant importance are Hayagriva Madhava Temple, Hako Powa Mecca (a prime Muslim Pilgrimage), Madan Kamdev Temple and Ganesha Temple. National Parks

Kaziranga National Park

Kaziranga National Park is situated at the banks of the mighty Brahmaputra River (called Tsang Po in few neighboring countries). It is at a distance of 217 Kms from Guwahati. Presence of many bus-stops (funded by Assam Government) makes it easy to get a cheap ride from Kaziranga National Park to Guwahati and other nearby cities. The park is 40 km wide. Much of it comprises of swamp and grasslands, hence making it perfect niche for one-horned Rihnoceros (the major tourist attraction) and thousands of migratory birds arrive at the park every year. According to Guwahati Travel Guide, the best time to visit the Kaziranga National Park is during late February and March. We at Guwahati Travel Guide, help you get the tickets for the busses at the best possible time and at the cheapest rates. Also, we provide cheap cabs for complete to-and-fro journey.

Manas National Park

Manas National Park is a UNESCO Natural World Heritage. It lies on the foothills of the majestic Himalyas. It is a Project-tiger Reserve, an Elephant Reserve and a biosphere reserve (all in one), which is considered as a unique distinction. The Manas River which flows through west of the park is split into 2 separate rivers (Beki and Bholkaduba) amidst the Park. This river is a main water resource for the whole park. Guwahati Travel Guide advices you to visit this park in the months of February and November. We provide all types of accomodation (with the help of Forest Department) to your stay more pleasant.

Assam State Zoo a.k.a. Botanical Garden

Assam State Zoo is the largest Zoo in whole North-East Region of India. It is famous for being habitat to Male Indian Peafowl, Single Horn Rinoceros, Indian Tiger, Pelicans, and Deers. A small botanical garden is also part of this zoo. Guwahati Travel Guide recommends you to visit Assam State Zoo in the month of February. Other Places

Saraighat Bridge

Saraighat Bridge was inaugurated by honourable the then Prime Minister Jawaharlal Nehru in 1962. Saraighat Bridge serves as a road in transit for tourists travelling from guwahati to other parts of Assam. It is built on mighty river Brahmaputra and hence it is one of the largest bridges in North East. Saraighat Bridge is famous for joining the north and south of Saraighat. Saraighat is considered as a place with significant historical importance because it witnessed a battle in which Ahoms (rulers of Assam) triumphed over Mughals (600 years ago).
